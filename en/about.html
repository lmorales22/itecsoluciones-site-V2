Practical advantages

Capabilities that impact operations, not just theory

These are practices we bring into projects and Fractional CIO engagements because they directly improve control, continuity, and efficiency for SMBs.

Automation as a competitive edge

We have built automations with n8n and Python for internal workflows and client operations. When we assess a company, we do not only look at infrastructure: we also look for repetitive manual work that consumes time and creates avoidable errors.

  • Reports someone builds manually every week.
  • Data copied from one system to another.
  • Alerts and checks never configured due to lack of time.

Every repetitive process is a measurable opportunity for savings and better operational control.

Verified continuity, not just promised continuity

A backup that is never tested is not a backup. It is a hope. We design the backup strategy, implement it, and verify restoration with documented evidence.

  1. 3-2-1 strategy (3 copies, 2 media types, 1 offsite).
  2. Technical implementation based on operational reality.
  3. Restoration testing and documented results.
  4. Fixes before a real incident forces the issue.

Continuity is managed as a process, not a sales line.

Vendor management with results

Many SMBs renew IT contracts automatically. iTec Soluciones reviews terms, benchmarks options, and renegotiates with cost, coverage, and operational risk in mind.

  • Internet, hosting, cloud, and licensing.
  • Support conditions and SLAs when applicable.
  • Prioritization based on business impact.

Well-executed first reviews often uncover savings and better terms without sacrificing continuity.

How we work

Principles designed to avoid surprises

Clarity before speed

We would rather spend more time planning than start execution without a clear scope. Most overruns come from poor definition, not lack of urgency.

Sales honesty

If you do not need what we offer, we say it. If a simpler solution works better, we will recommend that instead.

Documentation as a deliverable

Diagrams, inventories, credentials, configurations, and policies are documented for continuity and knowledge transfer.

Clear boundaries

We define what is included, excluded, and what changes scope or cost. If something changes, we communicate before execution.
